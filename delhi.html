History of Delhi

Delhi, the capital of India, holds a rich historical legacy that spans millennia. The city has been a witness to the rise and fall of numerous empires, including the Mughals, Delhi Sultanate, and the British Raj.

Good time to Visit

The best time to visit Delhi largely depends on your preferences regarding weather and events. Here are some general pointers:
1. Winter (October to March): This period is considered the best time to visit Delhi. The weather is pleasant during these months, with temperatures ranging from around 5°C (41°F) to 25°C (77°F). It's the peak tourist season as the climate is ideal for exploring the city without extreme heat. The city also hosts various cultural events, fairs, and festivals during this time.
2. Spring (February to March): Spring is also a good time to visit Delhi as temperatures begin to rise gradually, and the city blooms with colorful flowers.
3. Autumn (September to October): Just before winter, the weather is relatively pleasant, and the city is gearing up for festivals like Diwali, making it an exciting time to experience the cultural vibrancy of Delhi.
It's important to note that summer in Delhi (April to June) can be extremely hot with temperatures soaring well above 40°C (104°F), and the monsoon season (July to September) brings heavy rainfall and humidity. These periods might not be as comfortable for outdoor activities and sightseeing.
